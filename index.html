Welcome to Munnar!

Munnar, located in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India, is a stunning hill station renowned for its tea gardens, lush green landscapes, and pleasant climate. It's an ideal destination for nature lovers and those seeking a peaceful retreat.

Top Attractions

Tea Gardens of Munnar

Explore the sprawling tea estates of Munnar. The verdant rolling hills and tea plantations offer picturesque views and a chance to learn about tea production. You can take guided tours to see the tea-making process and enjoy fresh, aromatic tea right from the source.

Eravikulam National Park

Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, this park is known for its stunning landscapes and rich biodiversity. Don’t miss the Anamudi Peak, the highest point in South India. The park is perfect for trekking and wildlife enthusiasts, offering a chance to see unique flora and fauna.

Mattupetty Dam

The Mattupetty Dam is a scenic spot known for its tranquil waters and surrounding hills. Enjoy a boat ride or simply relax by the lakeside while taking in the serene atmosphere. The area is also known for its rich bird life, making it a great spot for birdwatchers.

Attukal Waterfalls

Attukal Waterfalls, situated between Munnar and Pallivasal, offers a breathtaking view of cascading water amidst lush greenery. It's a great place for nature walks and photography. The sound of the waterfall combined with the surrounding landscape creates a peaceful escape.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: The ideal time to visit Munnar is between October and March when the weather is cool and pleasant.
  • Local Cuisine: Sample local Kerala cuisine, including delicious dishes like Appam, Stew, and fresh seafood.
  • Weather Preparation: Munnar’s weather can be unpredictable. Pack layers and be prepared for occasional rain.
  • Transportation: Renting a car or hiring a local taxi is recommended for convenient travel around Munnar, as public transport options are limited.

Monsoon Experience

Visiting Munnar during the monsoon season is an entirely different experience. The lush greenery becomes even more vibrant, and the tea plantations and forests come alive with a fresh, verdant glow. The misty mornings and drizzling rain add a mystical charm to the landscape, making it a perfect time for those who enjoy the romance of rain and the tranquility it brings.

During the monsoon, the waterfalls are at their most majestic, and the rivers are full and fast-flowing. The rains can sometimes lead to road closures or travel delays, so it's essential to stay informed about weather conditions and travel with a flexible itinerary. Despite the occasional heavy showers, the cool weather and mist-covered hills create a magical atmosphere that's worth experiencing.
